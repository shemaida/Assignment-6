Iambic pentameter is the rhythm of Shakespearean verse. Each line has 10 syllables in the pattern of unstressed-stressed. Iambic pentameter helps guide the actor’s intentions and delivery, and also aids in memorization. However, like a heartbeat, the meter can change depending on the emotions of the character. It may speed up, slow down, skip a beat, or stop altogether.
